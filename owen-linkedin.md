## About

I'm a 2025-26 William Southam Journalism Fellow at Massey College. Before moving to Canada in 2018, I was a Beijing-based reporter covering Chinese politics and business trends. My writing has appeared in the Financial Times and The New York Times. I've also freelanced for Al Jazeera English, The BBC, The Washington Post, NPR, Channel NewsAsia, The Globe and Mail and the Toronto Star.

During a seven-year journalism career in China, I dodged bandits on a highway, snuck into a village in revolt over local corruption near the South China Sea and investigated why deodorant is a tough sell in China (hint: biology & cultural beliefs). Outside journalism, I've led communications, content strategy, and media relations across private and public sectors.

I love storytelling. Nothing excites me more than reading — or watching — a good story well told. When I'm not reading, I think a lot about how to write better.
