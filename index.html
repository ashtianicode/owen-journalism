Why Owen Isn't Getting Interviews Critical Fix

Root Cause Found: Owen's resume is split into "Communications Experience" and "Reporting Experience" — two non-chronological sections bucketed by category. Modern ATS systems (Workday, Greenhouse, Lever, iCIMS) are trained to parse reverse-chronological timelines. When experience is bucketed by category, the parser cannot correctly associate dates with roles, resulting in a garbled candidate profile. His NYT and FT roles may be completely invisible to ATS.
Current ApproachWhat's HappeningRecommended Fix
Two separate sections by category ATS can't parse non-standard headings One "Professional Experience" section
Non-chronological order Dates get garbled in ATS database Strict reverse-chronological order
Journalism described in journalism language Keywords don't match comms job postings All roles use comms-relevant language
No professional summary No narrative bridge for human readers 3–4 line summary connecting journalism + comms
Unknown keyword optimization Missing critical ATS match terms 15–25 comms-specific keywords per application
The good news: Owen's background is genuinely excellent for senior comms roles. The NYT/FT names are career accelerants. The problem is purely structural — once ATS can actually read his resume, his interview rate should increase materially.

The Warm Introduction Framework

Owen should never cold-apply to anything. Here's the system:

  1. Map the target: Identify the company and the hiring manager (usually VP/SVP Comms).
  2. Find the bridge: Search Owen's network for anyone connected to that person.
  3. Activate the bridge: "I noticed you're connected to [Name] at [Company]. Would you be comfortable making a brief introduction?"
  4. The informational meeting: Never ask for a job. Ask: "What does your team look for in a comms hire?"
  5. Follow up: Thank-you note within 24 hours. Share a relevant insight within a week.

Priority warm-intro targets: RBC (internal connections!), TD, Scotiabank, BMO, CPPIB, OTPP, Barrick Gold, McKinsey Toronto, BCG Toronto, Thomson Reuters, Global Affairs Canada, Asia Pacific Foundation.
